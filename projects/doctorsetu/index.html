01 · The problem

What DoctorSetu is trying to fix.

Indian prescriptions are handwritten, half-abbreviated, half-Latin. Elders forget which pill is for what. Chemists sometimes hand out the wrong strength. Nobody explains why you're taking three things instead of one.

DoctorSetu scans a prescription, decodes every medicine, and explains dosage, timing, and side effects in plain Hindi/English/Kannada. It also flags dangerous combinations before you get to the pharmacy.

Design principle
"A prescription only helps if the patient understands it."
02 · How it works

Three steps, no learning curve.

01

Snap the paper

Photograph the prescription or lab report. Handwritten, printed, or scanned — all fine.

02

Read every line

See each medicine with its purpose, dose, timing, and what to watch out for. Big text, no jargon.

03

Set reminders

Add pill reminders in one tap. Optional voice reminders in the elder's language of choice.
